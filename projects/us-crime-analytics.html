U.S. Crime Analytics (2020–2023)

Combined analysis of crime trends in Los Angeles and across the United States using advanced statistical methods.

Overview

The U.S. Crime Analytics project combines local and national perspectives on crime patterns across the United States from 2020 to 2023. At the local level, it explores crime data from Los Angeles, identifying patterns across different neighborhoods, weapon types, and time periods using advanced geospatial visualization techniques including heatmaps and interactive plots.

At the national level, it examines the complex interrelationships between economic indicators and violent crime rates across 217 major U.S. metropolitan areas, leveraging advanced statistical methodologies and machine learning techniques to explain 78.3% of the variance in violent crime rates through a combination of 27 distinct socioeconomic variables. This comprehensive approach provides a multi-dimensional view of crime trends, connecting local-level insights with broader national patterns to inform more effective public safety strategies and resource allocation.

Problem

Understanding crime patterns at both local and national levels presents significant challenges for law enforcement, policymakers, and researchers:

  • Local Perspective: Raw crime data from large metropolitan areas like Los Angeles is often overwhelming and difficult to interpret. Transforming this complex data into actionable insights requires identifying spatial and temporal patterns, weapon usage trends, and demographic correlations.
  • National Perspective: The relationship between economic conditions and criminal behavior represents one of the most persistent and consequential challenges in public policy. Previous studies have often relied on simplistic correlations or failed to account for confounding variables, leading to contradictory findings and ineffective policy recommendations.
  • Integration Challenge: Connecting local crime patterns with national socioeconomic trends requires sophisticated data integration and analysis techniques that can bridge different geographic scales and data sources.

This project addresses these challenges through a comprehensive analytical framework that integrates multiple data sources, advanced statistical methodologies, and domain expertise to provide actionable insights for stakeholders at both local and national levels.

Solution

Our solution is a comprehensive analytical framework that integrates multiple data sources and advanced statistical methodologies to provide insights into crime patterns at both local and national levels.

Interactive dashboard showing crime patterns across different geographic scales

The solution combines several key components:

  • Data Integration: We combined data from the Los Angeles Police Department, FBI Uniform Crime Reports, U.S. Census Bureau, Bureau of Labor Statistics, and Federal Reserve Economic Data to create a comprehensive dataset spanning local and national perspectives.
  • Advanced Analytics: We applied fixed-effects panel regression models, spatial regression techniques, time-series analysis, and machine learning algorithms to identify patterns and relationships in the data.
  • Interactive Visualization: We developed interactive dashboards using Folium for geospatial maps and D3.js for web-based visualizations, allowing users to explore crime patterns across different dimensions.

Process

Data Collection & Integration

Gathered and integrated data from multiple sources including LAPD, FBI, Census Bureau, BLS, and FRED, creating a comprehensive dataset spanning local and national perspectives.

Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

Standardized data formats, addressed missing values, and normalized variables to ensure consistency across different data sources and geographic scales.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Conducted initial statistical analysis and visualization to identify patterns, correlations, and potential insights in the data at both local and national levels.

Advanced Statistical Modeling

Applied fixed-effects panel regression, spatial regression, time-series analysis, and machine learning to identify relationships between variables and predict crime patterns.

Visualization & Reporting

Developed interactive dashboards and comprehensive reports to communicate findings and insights to stakeholders in an accessible and actionable format.

Key Features

Interactive Crime Heatmaps

Geospatial visualizations showing crime concentration across LA neighborhoods with filtering by crime type, time period, and demographic factors.

Time-Series Analysis

Temporal analysis of crime rates by hour, day, month, and year, revealing patterns and trends in criminal activity over time.

Multivariate Regression Models

Advanced statistical models identifying significant economic predictors of violent crime across 217 major U.S. metropolitan areas.

Demographic Analysis

Correlation studies between crime rates and socioeconomic factors, revealing differential impacts across regions and demographic groups.

Cross-Scale Integration

Tools connecting neighborhood-level data with metropolitan and national trends, providing a multi-dimensional view of crime patterns.

Policy Simulation

Framework for testing intervention strategies at different scales, allowing policymakers to evaluate potential impacts before implementation.
